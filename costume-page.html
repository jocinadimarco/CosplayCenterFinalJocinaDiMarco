What to know about Cosplay costumes!

The cosplay outfit itself is the first piece most people start thinking about when looking to cosplay a character. There are many ways that people go about this, from buying pre-made costumes, altering pre-made pieces, or building one from scratch. All of these are different options with their own pros and cons that each hold its place in the cosplay community. Let’s take a look at what these mean and how to go about them. Many cosplayers buy their costumes from an online cosplay shop, especially for more intricate but popular characters (I’m looking at you, gatcha game cosplayers). Buying a ready-made costume

There are a handful of popular and reputable only sellers when it comes to buying pre-made cosplayers. These sites are DokiDoki Cosplay, Uwowo Cosplay and MicCostumes which are all well known and used options in the cosplay community. These shops provide worldwide shipping, language setting and are updated with the latest popular characters ready to add to your cart. While being the most popular, these are not your only options. Many online shops provide their own cosplay sales, some with the same supplier as these well known brands. A commonly used alternative that can sometimes save money is buying from sellers on AliExpress or TaoBao as long as they have posted reviews.

Making or altering your costume

Buying cosplayers from online sellers is often expensive, with it being common for just the cosplay outfit itself to reach $100 before shipping. When buying pre-made cosplays, the ways to save money comes from sales, small sellers or secondhand marketplaces. Always make sure to double check your sizing in reference to their size chart as they differ across different countries and sellers.

Making or altering your costume

While many like wearing a fully pre-made cosplay, this is not the only way. The community is filled with many talented cosplayers that build their cosplays themselves using skills from sewing, metalwork, bedding, foam crafting and more. Building a cosplay from scratch can be daunting and a lot of work, especially when needed to learn new skills. Luckily there are many cosplayers and craft makers who love to share their skills. You can find resources such as patterns, tutorials and workshops that can all help you on your journey to create the cosplays of your dreams. For others looking for a less ambitious or cost effective way to cosplay, altering is a great way to cosplay. This can be done by leveling up a premade cosplay with upgrades and adjustments, or thrifting and upcycling similar pieces to resemble your desired character.
