Where to Start With Cosplay Props

Props can be a fun part of cosplaying, giving you something to interact with or to show off something that your character always brings around. This is a great way to bring characters to life, but it can be daunting to get started. Again, there are a few options that you could go about this so let’s get started.

Commissions and pre-made

When it comes to character props, they are often not sold by the popular cosplay shops that you might buy your cosplay from. You can find people taking commissions for cosplay props privately or on separate more broad cosplay websites.

For a much cheaper price most bought cosplay pops are perched on shops like Etsy as 3D prints, or even sold as the file itself for the buyer to print on their own. This usually only requires you to use some painting skills and there are many tutorials that are made specifically for this step.

Homemade

Like other parts of cosplay, props can be made completely by yourself from home with a few common materials. Some popular and well suited materials are:

  • Cardboard - lightweight
  • Craft or EVA foam - light and flexible
  • PVC pipe - durable but light structuring
  • Wood

Making props is a creative process and there is no right or wrong way to make your props from scratch. There and many prop-making videos to watch and don’t be afraid to taking away techniques and tricks from people that are even not necessary building the same props as you. Be creative but also be safe when working with your materials and wear safety gear when needed. A good thing to keep in mind with any props if ou plan on taking them to conventions and events. Check the rules of both your city and event as they have their own rules with both fake weapon carrying and even prop materials. Make sure to be following all rules so that your hard work does get you in trouble or get taken at the door.
