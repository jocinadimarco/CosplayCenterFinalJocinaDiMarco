Contact Lenses

Changing eyes are a popular thing in the cosplay community, wanting to look as much like a character as possible, or just to match their vibe. While some stop at makeup, a good amount of cosplayers use contacts as a way to complete their look, with enlarged or colours lenses. These lenses can be tricky and even scary at first, so here is some important things to know if you decide to give contacts a try for your next cosplay.

Safety

This is one of the things out of cosplay that is recommend not to go cheap with. Wearing cosmetic contacts come with risks and it is suggested to purchase them from a contact lens specific shop and to skip the listings you might find on AliExpress or Temu this time. You can find suggestions from different cosplayers online or make sure your sellers is safe with reviews and qualifications.

Once you get a hold of your contacts, there are still a few things to ensure that you are using them correctly and keeping your eyes safe. The first thing is that contacts are shipped in liquid that is not actually for your eyes; make sure to soak them in contact solution for the recommended time. When it comes to wearing the contacts lenses, you can actually end up putting them in upside down, which is not what you want. Take a carful look at the shape of the contact in your hand to make sure it is bent the right way. And finally, as a general rule of thumb, keep your hands clean, put them on before your makeup, only wear for the recommended amount of time and follow the rules listed for your lenses.
